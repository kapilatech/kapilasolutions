Military Health

Booz Allen works with of the Department of Defense and the Department of Veterans Affairs to proudly serve our clients in the Military Health System. Veterans represent over one third of our staff. We offer a unique set of capabilities and services that support our Military Health clients, including healthcare optimization and transformation, health information technology and electronic health record systems, privacy and security, clinical operations, brain and behavioral health services, and strategic planning, communications and change management. Our dedicated teams identify ways to become more efficient and effective, while improving the readiness, healthcare quality, outcomes, and access to care for military personnel and their families.

Clinical Operations


Booz Allen’s clinical operations are built on the foundation of a resilience based, patient-centered, interdisciplinary care approach. By integrating human performance and psychological health best practices our model addresses the holistic needs of military service members and their families.  We provide expertise across four integrated clinical operations capabilities.

  • Clinical service delivery
  • Infrastructure and operational support
  • Performance metrics and scientific analysis
  • Education and training

Program Evaluation

We have experts in cognitive and behavioral psychology, epidemiology, statistics, instructional design, public health, and public policy who support all stages of the program-evaluation life cycle—from engaging stakeholders to synthesizing and designing evaluations, gathering and analyzing data, and generating recommendations to inform programs and policies.

  • Data collection and tool design
  • Data storage, management, and security
  • Development of analytical frameworks
  • Research design and protocol

Health and Outcomes Analytics

Improving health outcomes is a central goal of today’s military health system.  We support our clients in measuring and analyzing outcomes in health and healthcare delivery, as well as in the areas of public-health and clinical-efficacy research.

  • Data collection, management, and quality assurance
  • Big data/cloud analytics (e.g., data mining, trending, predictive analytics, and visualization)
  • Advanced statistics, scientific analysis, and computational modeling
  • Policy/regulations mapping and development

Health Facility Transformation

Our expertise in health facility transformation optimizes capital investment by planning and managing infrastructure to support healthcare delivery with right-sized facilities and services based on a modeled market forecast that can reduce cost and improve quality.

  • Capital investment strategies
  • Energy-program and technology strategies
  • Facility management
  • Healthcare requirements analysis

Health Information Technology

Health Information Technology (HIT) continues to evolve as solutions become more sophisticated and organizations seek to achieve interoperability with a wide range of partners. We offer a variety of technology design and development solutions, including IT engineering, architecture design, data warehouse development, e-discovery, testing and evaluation, and mobile development and integration solutions.

  • Health information technology
  • Information assurance for systems
  • Information technology portfolio management
  • System development life cycle

Healthcare Information Privacy and Security

Our legal, regulatory, security, risk management, and training experts are focused on providing comprehensive solutions to the most pressing and complex health information privacy and security issues facing the Military Health System (MHS).

  • Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPPA) privacy and security
  • Integration, application, and implementation of federal and Department of Defense (DoD) privacy and security requirements
  • Program design, planning and governance
  • Training designs and techniques for diverse and mobile participants

Strategic Planning

In the changing environment of the Military Health System (MHS), agencies need a clear purpose and actionable future direction. Our strategic planning experts help organizations to set priorities, align energy and resources, improve process operations, and respond to changing dynamic environments.

  • Stakeholder analysis
  • Balanced scorecard and strategy mapping
  • Change management and communications
  • Process optimization

Strategic Communications

Our strategic communications professionals specialize in the targeted development and delivery of key messages that engage key stakeholders at the right time, in the right manner, and with the right responsiveness to drive behavior change and achieve business objectives.

  • Communication strategy, planning, and implementation
  • Health campaigns and promotion
  • Public relations and media outreach
  • Stakeholder relationship management

Transformation Communications

As agencies undergo major changes—such as realignment, introduction of new technology, or a full scale culture shift—our transformation experts plan and implement approaches integral to building ownership, changing behavior, addressing uncertainty, and reinforcing mission continuity.

  • Organizational transformation and change readiness
  • Stakeholder analysis and engagement
  • Change communications strategy, planning, and execution
  • Performance measure development, monitoring, and reporting

Performance Planning, Measurement, and Monitoring

We provide comprehensive performance planning and analytic expertise to support military health in care delivery (re)design, measurement and analysis, and outcomes improvement. Our experts conceive and develop customized tools to help organizations identify cost saving opportunities, assess return on investment (ROI), and improve quality and clinical currency.

  • Business case analysis and performance planning
  • Performance measure development, monitoring, and reporting
  • Decision support-tool design, development, and maintenance
  • Healthcare business-operations analytics

Conference Spotlight

7th Annual Conference on the Science of Dissemination & Implementation

Booz Allen will be showcasing its work in military health communications at the 7th Annual Conference on the Science of Dissemination and Implementation, December 8-9, 2014 in Bethesda, Md. Since October 2011, Booz Allen has supported the U.S. Navy in the design and implementation of the Navy’s flagship responsibly drinking campaign, “Keep What You’ve Earned.” The campaign uses affirmative messaging to remind Sailors of how hard they have worked and how much they have to lose. At the conference the team will present its health communications approach to tackling this important military readiness issue and share behavior change results and findings from campaign’s first year. The conference, co-hosted between the National Institutes of Health and AcademyHealth, aims to grow the research base by bridging the gap between evidence, practice, and policy in health and medicine.

  • A Glimpse into the Transformation of the Military Health System
    On January 2, 2013, the National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) was signed into law, leading to the creation of the Defense Health Agency (DHA) and the establishment of enhanced Multi-Service Markets (eMsM). Such legislation reflects a significant governmental shift to elevate the Military Health System (MHS) to a more integrated organization. This transition has the opportunity to improve the future healthcare of our nation’s military ranks, but will likely face a series of obstacles that require thoughtful, multifaceted solutions.
