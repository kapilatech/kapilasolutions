Presentation Schedule

Time Presentation Speakers
Monday, February 24 at 9:20 am Privileged Users Going Rogue: Preventing and Detecting Malicious Insiders
Recent insider incidents have put enterprises and government agencies on high alert. End-users with broad access rights, such as system admins, pose significant risks to organizations. Driven by fervent beliefs, national loyalty, or money, this type of user can cause massive data leaks or system sabotage. Is it possible to identify a potential threat? Are there warning signs? This panel will discuss managing the risks of malicious insiders including understanding behavioral aspects, implementing organizational practices, and coordinating effective incident response.

Dr. Stephen Band, Principal, Behavioral Intelligence Specialists and Former Chief Behavioral Science Unit, FBI

Dawn Cappelli, Director, Insider Risk Management, Rockwell Automation; and Founder of the CERT Insider Threat Center

Richard Hale, Deputy Chief Information Officer for Cybersecurity, Department of Defense

Mike McConnell, Facilitator, Vice Chairman, Booz Allen Hamilton; and former Director of National Intelligence
Monday, February 24

Note: This is a closed session.		 Elevating Cyber Risk from the Server Room to the Boardroom
When a cyber attack hits, every minute counts. Technology problems must be solved, but the true impact is to business confidence and continuity. Integrated real-time remediation is required. It’s the CEO who must understand the breadth of potential business impacts to make the right strategic decisions.		 Mike McConnell,  Vice Chairman, Booz Allen
Monday, February 24 at 1:15 pm Right Skills, Right Time, Right Industry: Women in Security
Women account for 11% of the global information security workforce, and their diverse voice and problem solving approaches represent an opportunity. This (ISC) 2 Global Information Security Workforce Study discusses how women have had an impact in the field of Cybersecurity.

Julie Peeler, Moderator, (ISC) Foundation Director, (ISC)2

Patricia Goforth, Officer MI Cyber, Booz Allen Hamilton

Cecily Joseph, Sr Director, Corporate Responsibility & Compliance at Symantec

Jennifer Napper, Director of Plans and Policy, J-5, US Cyber Command

Marene Allison, Global CISO and VP of Information Security at Johnson & Johnson
Tuesday, February 25 at 2:00 pm Building a Bunker for Business Assets and Processes
InfoSec's recent data analytics ignores both a lack of focused asset management, and the lack of actual system design and architecture in many organizations. Leveraging experience from online banking, we'll propose a bunker-like architecture for critical assets.		 Todd Inskeep, Senior Associate, Booz Allen
Wednesday, February 26 at 11:20 am A Comfy Couch for Critical Assets
Many organizations struggle to protect critical assets because they have failed to identify and prioritize those assets. Organizations need to better identify, risk rank, and prioritize protection of the assets that drive their profitability.		 Todd Inskeep, Senior Associate, Booz Allen
