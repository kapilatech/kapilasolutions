Elevating the Tools of the Trade: The New Value of E 2.0 Social Media and Change Agent Networks
Donna Lucas, Lead Associate, Strategy and Organization, Booz Allen Hamilton
Joyce Dubin, Strategy and Organization Consultant, Booz Allen Hamilton
Monday, April 15th, 4:45pm
Salon 5

Change agent networks and social media are like the yin and yang of the change management ecosystem – complementary catalysts in a dynamic change management program. As change professionals, we recognize the importance of developing networks and leveraging communications to champion change among stakeholders. But are we utilizing the change tools that meet the interests and needs of our audiences and clients? While it’s common knowledge that social media creates the opportunity for real-time, cross-country spontaneity and feedback loops, there is more these platforms can launch. This workshop provides an in-depth look at two change agent network initiatives grounded in IT innovation and leveraged through social media and other diverse communications tools. Participants will also gain a realistic perspective on social media considerations, overcoming resistance, and resource requirements required to address the importance of ongoing engagement. Booz Allen Hamilton’s Donna Lucas and Joyce Dubin will co-present and facilitate an engaging workshop on building change networks by leveraging social media and other communication tools. The presenters share their change agent network project experiences for both award-winning social media efforts at Booz Allen, as well the use of change networks and social media for a client in the Federal Government.

Setting the Stage: Change Management, Cloud and the Federal Government
Jennifer Bindhammer, Associate, Booz Allen Hamilton
Terence Mandable, Principal, Booz Allen Hamilton
Tuesday, April 16th, 11:15am
Salon 6,7

As monitors of the U.S. economy, “The Reporting Agency” and “The Statistics Agency” generate surveys, reports and market research reliant on huge data sets.* Yet, their legacy systems, manual processes and historical methodologies are no longer viable to meet the needs of federal or private-sector decision-makers; individuals and organizations used to making decisions in a matter of minutes anywhere, anytime, anyplace.  To better meet the needs of stakeholders, these agencies launched initiatives to move from these legacy systems and siloed networks to cloud-based systems. Both agencies acknowledged the need for change management, but only one implemented a full change management plan. In this session, we will compare and contrast these agencies, identify change management challenges to technical implementations and pinpoint “non-negotiable” change management components for a transition to the Cloud to be successful in the federal space.
* Agency names have been changed for client confidentiality.

Measuring and Visualizing the Return on Change Management
Daniel Liebermann, Associate, Booz Allen Hamilton
Tuesday, April 16, 2:45pm
Salon 4

Organizations are increasingly interested in understanding the return on investment (ROI) that comes from adopting a change management strategy. This presentation explores new ideas regarding how to measure and visualize the ROI of change management. The speaker will introduce new measurement approaches and visual tools that can be used to induce adoption and monitor the effectiveness of a change management strategy. This Pecha Kucha presentation will consider the perspective of individuals within an organization as they use these tools to understand the value of their investment.

Coaching Leaders through Change
Janeen Latini, Senior Associate, Booz Allen Hamilton
Lori Zukin, Principal, Booz Allen Hamilton
Wednesday April 17, 8:30am
Platinum Salon F-J

There exists an extensive library of models and best practices for how organizations and leaders should lead change. There is little information, however, on what a leader must do to change him/herself. For change to truly occur in an organization, we must focus not only on the external actions a leader must take, but also on the internal changes he/she must experience. We put great expectation and demands on leaders, but give insufficient attention to their needs. This session focuses on supporting leaders to thrive as individuals, leaders, and change agents. Through a review and connection of Change Management and Leadership Coaching principles, we have developed an interactive session that illustrates how to help leaders experience their own change and be better prepared to model and lead change within their organizations. This session may be of interest to: HR/Talent Management professionals, change team leaders, consultants, and coaches of change leaders.
